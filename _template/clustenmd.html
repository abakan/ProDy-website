ENM-Based Sampling of Essential Conformational Space at Full Atomic Resolution

ClustENMD is a hybrid method that performs unbiased conformational sampling of highly flexible and/or large biomolecular systems (Kurkcuoglu et al., 2016; Kaynak et al., 2021) with high computational efficiency. The major steps of the algorithm iteratively executed in each generation are:
(i) deforming each conformer (from previous generation) along random combinations of global ANM modes for generating new conformers,
(ii) clustering of new conformers to find cluster representatives, and
(iii) performing a short molecular dynamics (MD) simulation for each cluster representative using OpenMM software.

Features

You can use ClustENMD for

  • Unbiased sampling of conformers starting from a folded structure (experimental or modeled).
  • Obtaining information on transition intermediates/paths connecting two states.
  • Generating 2-D density distributions of conformers/energy landscapes.
  • Comparison with experimental structures using principal component analysis.
  • Generating conformers for ensemble docking purposes, as input for protein-ligand, protein-protein and protein-DNA docking (Kurkcuoglu and Doruker, 2016; Can et al., 2017; Kurkcuoglu and Bonvin, 2020).
